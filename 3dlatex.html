\section{Introduction}
Medications are used in several treatments. Its useful to know the blood concentration given a half life, dosage and weight. We can even find blood concentration of nutrients with this formula. 


$$D=Doseagemg/(Weightkg*0.068)$$
D is in ug/ml. It assumes 5 liters of blood for a weight of 80 kg, where the exact coefficient of volume comes from Wikipedia.

$$x=(x+D)e^{-t/\tau}$$
$$x=De^{-t/\tau}/(1-e^{-t/\tau})$$
$$Avg=\int_{0}^{24}(x+D)e^{-t/\tau}dt/24$$
$$t_{1/2}=\tau/\ln 2$$
$$Avg=Dt_{1/2}/16.63$$
$$Avg (ug/ml)=Dosagemg/Wieghtkg*t_{1/2}/1.131$$
where dosage is in mg, weight in kg, $t_{1/2}$ in hrs. If the percentage absorption is $P$ , 
$$Avg (ug/ml)=Dosagemg/Wieghtkg*t_{1/2}/1.131*P$$

If there are $N$ doses, its simply multiplied by it.
$$Avg (ug/ml)=N*Dosagemg/Wieghtkg*t_{1/2}/1.131*P$$
An example would be 500 mg , 1 dose per day with a 70\% absorption, a body weight of 80 kg and a half life of 14 hrs. It gives a blood level of 54.16 ug/ml if taken daily. This reading was verified with a real blood test for valproate within 10\% error.
